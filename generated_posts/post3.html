What is Kafka? 🤔

Apache Kafka is an open-source distributed event streaming platform used by thousands of companies for high-performance data pipelines, streaming analytics, data integration, and mission-critical applications.

Let's break it down!

Let's assume you are Santa 🎅, and are responsible for delivering gifts 🎁 to all the children on Christmas. You have the following resources at your disposal:

-🧝 Elves: They are responsible for getting the gift requests from the children to Santa(you), they help out in the workshops to make gifts and package them as well. -📝 Wish Lists: They contain the information about what gift the child has requested and the type of gift it is i.e., "Toys", "Gadgets" or maybe "Games". -🦌 Reindeers: They are Santa's personal couriers, they pass on information to Santa and also bring the gifts to Santa when the elves finish making them. -🏭 Workshop: Santa's workshop is where all the work is down from making the gifts to packaging them.

In a traditional setting, You follow a first in first out (FIFO) approach just like a queue, the Elves put the wish list they get from children inorder and each gift request is processed in the same order. You then pull out one gift request and then processes it by sending it to the workshop where each gift request is handled one at a time. You now face a backlog of gift requests and are overwhelmed by the amount of incoming gift requests and delays are innevitable now (imagine a child receiving the gift after christmas). You've no choice but to optimize the process and increase the number of gifts processed at a given time.

Here comes the magic of Kafka

Producers publish messages/data to the Kafka. Elves are now producers, and they publish all the gift requests to Kafka Topics are streams of messages of a particular type. Wish lists are now topic, and they segreggate all the requests into different topics like "Toys", "Gadgets" and "Games". This helps you organize and categorize wishlists/messages. Broker is a server that stores, manages, and distributes data streams.

Your reindeer's are the brokers, responsible for carrying the messages (gift requests) between different departments. They ensure that messages reach the right destinations and are handled appropriately. A consumer can subscribe to one or more topics from the brokers, and consume the subscribed messages by pulling data from the brokers. You(Santa) are the consumer in this case, you subscribe to the different topics (wish lists) and receives messages about the gifts that need to be prepared and delivered. To balance load, a topic can be divided into multiple partitions and each broker stores one or more of those partitions. You now divide your worshop into partitions each partition is responsible for a separate task. Each workshop (partition) focuses on a specific type of gift, ensuring that the elves are efficiently working on their assigned tasks.
